NextGen CM/ECF

This Court uses the NextGen version of the Case Management/Electronic Case Filing System (CM/ECF). NextGen simplifies electronic filing by combining CM/ECF and PACER accounts, enabling filers to use one login and password to electronically file in all NextGen courts where they have been granted permission to file.

Start Filing Electronically in NextGen

► Link Your Upgraded PACER account to your ECF Account

Users need both an upgraded PACER login and password, and an ECF login and password, in order to link your accounts and begin electronic filing in NextGen. If you have not obtained an individual PACER account, or if you have your own PACER account but you haven't upgraded it, see directions below.

► Instructions for linking your upgraded PACER account to your CM/ECF Account

► Need help with your PACER account?  

      Email pacer@psc.uscourts.gov or call (800) 676-6856

► Need help with your CM/ECF account?  

      Seattle call 206-370-5200

      Email*: ECFHelp_Seattle@wawb.uscourts.gov

      Tacoma call 253-882-3900,

      Email*: ECFHelp_Tacoma@wawb.uscourts.gov

      *When contacting ECFHelp, please include your name, bar number and account email address   so that we can assist you faster.

      You can also reach us via live chat, Chat "Live" with Court Staff

 

Filers with an ECF Account and a PACER account created BEFORE August 11, 2014:

Individual PACER accounts created prior to August 11, 2014, must be upgraded. PACER accounts that have not been updgraded will not be compatible with NextGen.

  • Instructions for upgrading your PACER account
  • Upgrade your PACER Account

Filers with an ECF Account but NO PACER Account:

You must have your own individual PACER account in order to file electronically. Your PACER account must be linked to your ECF filing account. [These documents are currently linked onthe live website]

  • Instructions for registering for a PACER account
  • Register for a PACER Account

Filers with No ECF Account and NO PACER Account:

Each applicant for an ECF account must first obtain an account and login through PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records).  See www.pacer.gov.  Through PACER, the applicant will request the appropriate type of access to the Court's ECF filing system.
