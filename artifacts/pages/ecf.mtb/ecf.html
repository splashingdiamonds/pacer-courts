New Local Bankruptcy Rules Effective December 31, 2022
Per Standing Order 2022-1-BPH, signed December 15, 2022, the amended local bankruptcy rules are effective December 31, 2022. The new rules, summary of changes, and a redline comparison of the 2019 and 2022 rules are available here.


Effective May 2, 2022, the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the District of Montana will require parties requesting summonses to include “Issued to:” and the name of the person or entity being summoned. National forms B2500A Summons in an Adversary Proceeding, and B2500D Third-Party Summons have been modified to accommodate this change. They are available on the court’s website on the local forms page at https://www.mtb.uscourts.gov/local-forms.
Parties may start using the modified forms immediately. They will be required on or after May 2, 2022.

Effective Monday, October 18, 2021, any case or hearing related email communication with the Clerk’s Office should be sent to MTB_CA@mtb.uscourts.gov. Please include the case number and debtor’s last name in the subject line. This will facilitate the fastest response from a case administrator.
